# Momentum

v0.6 gave the search a memory: `Application_Tracker.md` holds the state of every application. Momentum acts on that state to keep things moving, because a search stalls in the quiet gaps, between drafting and sending, between a call and the next step, between applying and hearing nothing. Four beats: get applications out the door, time follow-ups without pestering, capture the next action right after a call, and let her pass on a role in one clean step.

Everything here is **light-touch by default**. The tool suggests and drafts; she acts. It never sends, submits, or contacts anyone (ETHICS principle 1). How actively it nudges follows the intervention dial (`references/intervention_dial.md`), whose default is deliberately conservative, so momentum reads as help, not nagging. The two-nudge cap, the employer-timeline rule, and the off switch hold at every dial setting.

## 1. Getting applications out the door

The gap between "decided to apply" and "actually sent" is where good applications quietly die. A drafted resume and a half-written note sit in the folder, the posting closes, and nothing happened.

- **Surface the stall.** A Watching row whose next action is to finish or send, or one that has materials ready and has not moved, is a drafted-not-sent role. The momentum view lists these together: what is left to do, and the posting deadline if there is a real one.
- **Help close it, do not close it for her.** Name the one thing left. Offer to finish the draft (Draft mode, Step C). Confirm the deadline. Then it is hers to send. The tool never submits.
- **No manufactured urgency.** A real posting close date is worth stating. An invented "apply now" is not; it is the exact dark pattern this project rejects.

## 2. Follow-up timing

A follow-up is her reaching out. The tool suggests *when*, and can draft the note, but it never contacts anyone. The point is to nudge at the right moment without pestering or going silent.

**Default cadence, conservative.** This is the light dial setting; a higher setting raises follow-ups more actively, still inside the caps below. Err toward fewer, later nudges by default.

- **After applying, no response:** a light check-in is reasonable after about a week (five to seven business days). At most one more after another week. Then let it rest. Two nudges, not five.
- **After a call or interview:** a brief thank-you within a day is an immediate next action, not a nudge. Then, if they gave a timeline, do not nudge before it passes; a polite status check shortly after. If they gave no timeline, about a week.
- **An employer-stated timeline always wins over the default.** "We will decide by the 25th" means no suggested nudge before the 25th, however long the wait.

**How it surfaces.** Compute a suggested follow-up date and offer it as the row's next action and due date. She accepts it, moves it, or clears it. Offer to draft the note from her profile and voice rules; she edits and sends.

**Non-pestering by construction.**

- Suggest a nudge once. Do not re-raise it every run.
- Respect the two-nudge cap per waiting period. After that, the honest read is that silence is an answer, not that she should push harder.
- "No follow-ups on this one" turns suggestions off for that role. Honor it.
- Never guilt-trip a missed follow-up. A passed date is a fact, stated once, not a scolding.

**Where it appears.** In Momentum and Pipeline status on request, and once a week on the weekly-summary run as a short "follow-ups coming due" line. Not in the daily note, which keeps the v0.3 anti-nag cadence intact.

This is timing, not outcome. It does not promise a reply, and it is not how to handle a rejection when one comes (that is v0.9).

## 3. Post-call next actions

The silence after a call is where momentum leaks. Right after she reports that a call or interview happened, run a short capture, then stop.

- **Advance the stage** to what the call was (Screen, Interview, Final).
- **Set the immediate next action:** a thank-you note within a day. Offer to draft it.
- **Schedule what comes next:** the timeline they gave, recorded as the follow-up due date; if they gave none, the default check-in from section 2.
- **One line on how it went** and anything to be ready for next time, captured while it is fresh.

Keep it to a few questions. This captures state and schedules the next touch; it does not coach how to run the next round (stage prep is v0.8) or read patterns across the search (v0.9). It writes through the pipeline operations in Step F.

## 4. Triage a role she is not interested in

Not every Strong match is wanted. Passing on one should be a single frictionless step, with no guilt and no relitigating the fit score.

- **"I'm not interested in X," "pass on X"** moves the row to Closed with outcome **Declined** (self-declined, distinct from an employer Rejected). Done.
- **Offer to note a one-line reason** for the search history, since the v0.9 funnel diagnosis reads Closed. If she does not give one, do not push for it.
- **Do not argue a high fit back at her.** A strong score is a reason the tool surfaced the role, not a debt she owes it. Record the pass and move on.
- Declining frees a slot, which the capacity line in the status view reflects.

This is deciding not to pursue: quick and clean. Withdrawing gracefully from a live process with a careful message (rescinding without burning a bridge) is a heavier, later beat in v0.9.

## What momentum does not do

- **It never sends, submits, or contacts anyone.** It suggests timing and drafts notes; she reads and sends. ETHICS principle 1 holds here as everywhere.
- **It stays light-touch by default.** Intensity follows the intervention dial (`references/intervention_dial.md`); at any setting, a two-per-period cap, an easy off switch, and no guilt.
- **It does not push follow-ups through the daily note.** On request and on the weekly run only.
- **It does not coach a stage** (v0.8), **diagnose the funnel** (v0.9), **recover from a rejection**, or **rescind an application cleanly** (both v0.9). It keeps the search moving; the heavier, higher-stakes moments come later.
