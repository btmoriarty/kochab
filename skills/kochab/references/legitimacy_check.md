# Legitimacy Check

A quick screen on each new posting before it reaches her, so the scan does not send her chasing a ghost job, a scam, or a staffing-firm repost dressed as a direct hire. This runs as part of link verification; it is fast, and most postings pass.

The point is not to be suspicious of everything. It is to catch the few postings that waste an applicant's time or worse, and to flag them honestly rather than rank them as if they were real.

## What to check

- **Is the employer real and currently operating?** A findable company with a real presence, not a shell. For a name you cannot verify at all, say so.
- **Is the req live and recent?** A posting that has been up for months, or that reappears on a cycle, is often a pipeline-filler or a role that is not really open. Note the age if you can find it.
- **Does the comp band make sense?** A band far above or below the market for the title, or an implausibly wide band, is a flag. Not disqualifying, but worth naming.
- **Is this a direct hire or a staffing-firm repost?** Many "postings" are agencies advertising a role they hope to place into. Legitimate, but a different process, and often the same underlying job posted many times. Say which it is.
- **Scam signals.** Asks for payment, personal financial details, or an interview conducted entirely over text/chat app; a domain that mismatches the company; a too-good-to-be-true pitch. These get flagged plainly.

## How to report it

Attach a short line to any posting that is not a clean direct, live, real role. Do not bury a real concern, and do not manufacture one.

- Clean posting: no note needed.
- Aged or recurring: "Posted [date]; has been live a while, may be a pipeline-filler."
- Staffing firm: "This is [agency] advertising the role, not [employer] directly."
- Cannot verify the employer: "Could not confirm this company is currently operating; treat as UNVERIFIED."
- Scam signal: name the specific signal and recommend skipping.

If a posting fails badly enough to be a likely scam, it does not go in the ranked list at all; it goes in a short "screened out as likely not real" note so she can see it was checked.

## Guardrail

A legitimacy flag is a caution, not a verdict, except for clear scams. A staffing-firm repost or an older posting can still be worth a real application. Give her the fact and let her decide.
