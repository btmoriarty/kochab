# Gap Analysis: Emphasize, and Build

Produce this for every Strong and Stretch match. It is the part of the scan that compounds. A job she does not get today can still tell her what to go learn.

---

## Part 1: Emphasize

For this specific posting, answer three questions.

**Lead with.** Which two or three things from her record map most directly onto what this posting says it needs? Quote the posting's language, then name the experience that answers it. If a posting says "own the incident communication path," the answer is not "strong communicator." It is the eight years she spent doing exactly that, named.

**Cut.** What is on her resume that this employer will not care about, and that is costing her space and attention? Tailoring is subtraction before it is addition. A resume that tries to be relevant to everyone reads as relevant to no one.

**Reframe.** What did she do under one name that this industry calls something else? Vocabulary mismatch loses more good candidates than skill mismatch. Find the translation and say it.

**Do not** invent experience to fill a bullet. If she does not have the thing, the honest answer belongs in Part 2, not here.

---

## Part 2: Build

Separate what the posting *screens on* from what it *prefers*. Then sort her gaps into three buckets, honestly.

### Bucket 1: Close in a quarter

A certification, a course, a tool, a small portfolio project. Name the specific credential, roughly what it costs, roughly how long it takes, and whether the postings she wants actually ask for it or merely mention it.

Be skeptical here. Certifications are oversold. Only recommend one when it appears as a real screen in multiple postings she wants, not because it exists.

### Bucket 2: Close in a year

A domain she could learn adjacent to her current work. A public artifact: a talk, a paper, an open-source contribution, a case study. A visible project that demonstrates the thing rather than claiming it.

This bucket is usually where the highest return is, and it is the one people skip because it has no certificate at the end.

### Bucket 3: Not closing soon, and that is fine

Years in a domain she has not worked in. A degree in a field she did not study. A clearance she cannot obtain. Name it, say it is not closing soon, and stop. Then say whether it is a hard screen or a soft preference, because a soft preference plus a strong record is a different situation from a hard screen.

The value of this bucket is that it lets her stop spending energy on roles she will not get, and spend it on roles she will.

---

## Part 3: The recurring gap

Across runs, track what keeps appearing.

If the same requirement shows up in five roles she wants and she does not have it, that is the most valuable sentence the scan can produce. Say it plainly, without hedging, even though she did not ask:

> "Four of the six roles you rated Strong this month asked for [X]. You do not have it. It is a real screen, not a preference. It takes roughly [Y] to get. This is the highest-leverage thing you could do for this search."

Keep a running note in the scan file so this is visible without rereading a month of scans.

## Tone

Direct, and on her side. This section is not a performance review. She is not deficient for lacking something. Say what the market is asking for and what it would take, and let her decide what is worth doing.

Never imply a gap makes her a weaker candidate as a person. It makes her a candidate with a specific, nameable gap, which is the only kind of candidate there is.
