# The Intervention Dial

How much the tool coaches, nudges, and diagnoses is her setting, not the tool's default temperament. Some people want a quiet assistant that speaks when asked; others want an active coach that surfaces the next move. The dial makes that a choice she controls, light-touch by default, tunable up or down at any time.

It is a spine theme, not a single feature: it governs behavior across several modes rather than living in one.

## The setting

One value, stored in `Master_Profile.md` under an **Intervention level** line, captured at setup and changeable on request. Three positions:

- **Light (default).** The tool holds state and answers when asked. It does not push follow-ups, funnel reads, or encouragement on its own beyond the established weekly touch. This matches how Kochab behaved before the dial existed, so the default changes nothing for anyone who does not touch it.
- **Standard.** The tool offers the next action more actively: it may surface a due follow-up or a brief funnel observation on the weekly-summary run, and it nudges a little more readily, still within the anti-nag cadence.
- **High.** The tool coaches. It proactively raises follow-ups as they come due, offers a funnel read more often, and leans into encouragement where it is warranted. Still never at the cost of honesty, and still never in the daily note past the weekly touch.

She sets it by saying so ("be more hands-on," "dial the coaching back," "just hold things and let me ask"). Reflect the change back and update the profile line.

## What it governs

- **Momentum follow-up intensity** (`references/momentum.md`). The default cadence is the light setting; higher settings raise follow-ups more actively, always inside the two-nudge cap and the employer-timeline rule. The caps and the off switch hold at every setting.
- **Funnel diagnosis proactivity** (`references/process_health.md`). Light runs it only on request; higher settings may add a brief weekly read. The small-sample honesty holds at every setting.
- **The encouragement nudge** (`references/network.md`). The optional, honest nudge for chronically under-confident users is dial-governed: off at the light default, available at standard or high or when she opts in, and only on a role she genuinely qualifies for (Strong or clears the screens). It names a warm contact to lower the barrier, never pushes her toward a role she cannot do.

## What it does not change

The dial changes *how much* the tool speaks, never *whether it tells the truth*. It does not soften a gap, inflate a fit, manufacture a match, or push past an honesty guardrail at any setting. It does not turn off the ETHICS floor: no auto-submit, no fabrication, no dark patterns, at light or high. And it never overrides an explicit "no follow-ups on this one" or pushes coaching on someone in genuine distress. A higher setting is a more present assistant, not a less honest one.
