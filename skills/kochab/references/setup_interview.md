# Setup Interview

Ask one question at a time. Let her answer before moving on. Reflect back what you heard in her own words, not in resume language.

The whole interview should feel like a conversation with someone who is paying attention, not an intake form.

## Part 1: The resume, read back to her

Before asking anything new, read the resume and come back with three or four specific observations. This earns the rest of the interview.

- "You spent six years at X and the bullet is one line. What was that actually?"
- "This title says manager. Were you managing people, or managing the work?"
- "You list this tool. Did you use it, build with it, or run the team that did?"

## Part 2: Direction

1. What are the two or three kinds of work you actually want to be doing next?
2. What are you trying to move away from? (This is often more informative than the first answer.)
3. Is there a version of your career you have been talked out of that you still want?

## Part 3: Constraints, asked plainly

4. Where do you live, and what is the furthest you would commute, honestly, on a Tuesday in February?
5. On-site, hybrid, or remote? If hybrid, how many days is too many?
6. Would you relocate? For what?
7. What is your compensation floor? Not your target, your floor.
8. **When comp and mission conflict, which wins?** Answer this one for real, because every ranking in every scan depends on it.
9. What is a hard no? Industries, company stages, travel, on-call, managing people, not managing people.
9b. How many live applications can you give real attention to at once? Not a limit the tool enforces, just the number past which things start slipping. This becomes the capacity target in your tracker; the pipeline view flags when you cross it. If no number comes to mind, that is fine and we leave it unset.

## Part 4: Level

10. Would you take a title step down for better work? How far?
11. Would you take a step up you are not certain you are ready for?

Both answers matter. Many good roles are filtered out by an unexamined assumption about titles.

11b. Realistically, how many hours a week could you give to learning something new, if a gap kept blocking roles you wanted? This sets the pace of any study plan later; an honest small number beats an aspirational large one. Record it in the profile.

11c. How hands-on do you want this tool to be: hold everything and answer when you ask (light), offer the next move a bit more actively (standard), or actively coach and nudge (high)? This is the intervention dial; light is the default and you can change it any time. Record it as the Intervention level in the profile. See `references/intervention_dial.md`.

## Part 5: Honesty guardrails

Introduce this section explicitly: *"I am going to write drafts in your voice. I need to know where the resume is generous, so I never write a sentence you would have to walk back in an interview."*

12. Where were you the owner, and where were you adjacent to the owner?
13. Which technology do people assume you built, that you actually used, bought, or managed?
14. Which title on here overstates the scope? Which one understates it?
15. Is there a credential people assume you have that you do not?
16. Is there anything on this resume you would be uncomfortable being asked about in detail?

Turn each answer into a rule: **say X, never say Y.**

## Part 6: Voice

17. Are there words or phrases you would never write?
18. Do you have two or three things you have written that sound like you? (Emails count.)
19. How do you want to come across in a cover note: warm, precise, direct, understated?

## Part 7: Confirm

Read the draft profile back in summary. Ask what is wrong. Something always is.

## Part 8: Seed the story bank

Move on to `story_bank.md`. Pull story stubs from the resume, let her pick three to five, and interview each into a full situation-task-action-result story with its honest framing. This does not have to be exhaustive at setup; a few strong stories is enough to start, and it grows as she applies.

Then, and only then, move on to building the target-employer list.
