### The curriculum rebuilt around backward design
**Demonstrates:** designing a full course from objectives and assessments, not just delivering lessons.
**Situation:** Whetstone's 9th-grade biology results were flat and the units were a pile of activities with no through-line from goals to tests.
**Task:** Rebuild the year so assessments actually measured the stated objectives.
**Action:** Reworked all eight units with Understanding by Design: wrote the essential questions and end-of-unit assessments first, then built the lessons backward to them, and added common rubrics.
**Result:** Two years of steadier, more comparable results across sections, and three colleagues adopted the unit maps.
**Honest framing:** She designed it for her classroom and department; it was not published or sold. Say "designed and taught," never "published curriculum designer."
**Best for:** instructional-design roles, backward-design and assessment questions, "walk me through how you build a course."

### The Storyline pilot modules
**Demonstrates:** taking a lesson from paper to a working interactive e-learning module.
**Situation:** The district ran a blended-learning pilot and needed self-paced modules for two hard biology topics.
**Task:** Build interactive modules students could work through without a teacher in the room.
**Action:** Storyboarded cell division and ecosystems, built both in Articulate Storyline with knowledge checks and branching feedback, and revised them from student results.
**Result:** Both modules are still used by three teachers two years later.
**Honest framing:** Self-taught, intermediate Storyline. Say "built two modules in a pilot," never "expert in Storyline." Two modules is the whole portfolio so far.
**Best for:** e-learning and authoring-tool questions, portfolio-sample discussion, the classroom-to-ID pivot.

### The assessment-redesign committee
**Demonstrates:** moving a group of peers to a shared standard without authority over them.
**Situation:** The six-person science department graded the same courses very differently.
**Task:** Get the team onto common assessments and rubrics.
**Action:** Coordinated the redesign committee, ran the working sessions, built the shared rubric bank, and set up a simple reteach-after-data cycle.
**Result:** Common rubrics across all sections and a repeatable data-to-reteach routine.
**Honest framing:** One department in one school. Say "coordinated a six-teacher committee," never "led a district-wide initiative."
**Best for:** stakeholder and facilitation questions, change-without-authority, working with subject-matter experts.
