# Demo Script: Kochab

*A shot-by-shot script for a screen-recording walkthrough, roughly five minutes. Each row is one beat: what is on screen, and what to say over it. Keep the voice calm and plain; the tool's whole promise is honesty, so the demo should not oversell.*

**Setup before recording:** have a real (or realistic sample) resume ready, an empty folder to use as the search workspace, and the skill installed. Do a dry run of the setup interview first so you are not answering the honesty questions cold on camera.

---

## 0. Cold open (~15s)

- **On screen:** A generic job board with dozens of tabs open, or a cursor hovering over a "Easy Apply" button.
- **Say:** "Most job-search tools help you apply to more jobs, faster. This one is built on the opposite idea: apply to fewer, better, and never let the tool pretend on your behalf."

## 1. What it is (~25s)

- **On screen:** The repo README, scrolled to the "What's inside" section.
- **Say:** "It's a set of skills that run inside Claude. It finds roles that actually fit, tells you the truth about the ones that don't, and drafts your materials without inventing anything. One rule underneath all of it: it drafts, you send. It never submits an application for you."

## 2. Install (~20s)

- **On screen:** Saving the `kochab.skill` file, then connecting an empty folder as the workspace.
- **Say:** "Install is one step: save the skill. Then make a folder for your search and point Claude at it. Everything about your search lives in that folder, on your machine. Nothing personal goes anywhere else."

## 3. Setup and the honesty questions (~45s)

- **On screen:** Typing "set up my job scan," attaching the resume; the interview beginning; then the honesty-questions portion.
- **Say:** "You start by saying 'set up my job scan' and handing over your resume. It reads it back to you and asks about the parts that look compressed or inflated. Then it asks the question that makes the whole thing trustworthy" — *pause on the honesty question* — "for each thing on your resume that sounds more impressive than it was, tell me the honest version. Your answers become hard rules. From then on it will never write a sentence you'd have to walk back in an interview."

## 4. Story bank and target list (~25s)

- **On screen:** The story-bank seeding step; then the generated `Target_Employers.md`.
- **Say:** "It pulls a few real stories from your resume and helps you shape them. Those get reused in cover notes and interview prep later. Then it researches the employers who hire people like you, and writes them down as a starting list it will grow every run."

## 5. Schedule the scan (~15s)

- **On screen:** Pasting the handed-off prompt into a scheduled task, set to daily.
- **Say:** "Setup ends by handing you a block of text. Drop it into a scheduled task, set it daily, and you're done. It runs every morning on its own."

## 6. Reading a scan result (~45s)

- **On screen:** A scan note open, showing a ranked role with its fit score and gap.
- **Say:** "Here's a morning's result. Each role gets a fit score, but look at how it's shown: six sub-scores you can see and argue with, not a mystery number out of a hundred. It's rated Strong, Stretch, or Skip, with the real gap named" — *point* — "and a why-you-fit line right beside it, so it's honest in both directions. It's also checked each posting for legitimacy, so a ghost job or a scam never reaches this list."
- **On screen:** Scroll to a day that says "nothing new today."
- **Say:** "And most mornings look like this: nothing new. That's the tool working. If it found three exciting matches every day, you'd stop reading it."

## 7. Draft mode, the cover note (~30s)

- **On screen:** Typing "draft a note for [role]"; the resulting short email.
- **Say:** "When you want to apply, it drafts the note. Watch what it doesn't do: it never restates the job description. No 'I am writing to express my interest,' no reciting the requirements. It opens on one true, specific thing about the company, carries one of your real stories, and stops. And if you have no genuine reason to want the job, it tells you, instead of faking enthusiasm."

## 8. Plan mode, the study plan (~25s)

- **On screen:** A weekly summary surfacing a recurring-gap offer; then a generated study plan.
- **Say:** "When the same requirement keeps blocking roles you want, it says so, and offers a plan to close it. The plan is real resources with honest timelines, ending in something you can show, not just a certificate. It only offers this for a gap that's actually recurring, so it's not sending you to chase a credential for one job."

## 9. Interview mode (~25s)

- **On screen:** Typing "prep me for my [company] interview"; the resulting prep sheet.
- **Say:** "Once you land a screen, it preps you against the actual posting. The useful part is the mapping: here's a question they're likely to ask, and here's your real story that answers it. Where you're thin, it tells you honestly and gives you a composed answer, instead of a bluff."

## 10. Opportunity-brief (~20s)

- **On screen:** Pasting a recruiter message into the `opportunity-brief` skill; the resulting brief.
- **Say:** "There's a companion skill for the other direction. A recruiter reaches out, or a friend mentions a company, and you get a grounded brief: what the place actually is, whether it's stable, what the role really asks, and where your honest gaps are."

## 11. Close (~20s)

- **On screen:** The ETHICS.md file, then the repo landing page.
- **Say:** "Everything it does follows a few rules it won't break: it never applies for you, never scrapes around a site's terms, never fabricates, and never dresses up a guess as a measurement. It's a sharp assistant to someone still doing their own thinking. That's the whole point."

---

## Timing summary

Roughly 4:45 at a calm pace. To cut it shorter, drop beats 4 and 10; the core story is 1, 3, 6, 7. To go longer, let the setup interview and a real scan result breathe.

## Recording notes

- Use a sample profile, never a real person's private data, in anything you publish.
- The honesty questions (beat 3) and the "nothing new today" moment (beat 6) are the two beats that distinguish this from every other tool. Give them room.
- Do not speed-run the cover note. Letting viewers read a draft that clearly is not the job description restated is more persuasive than saying so.
